About me

Hi, I’m Noam—a game sound designer and interactive audio specialist with a creative tech background. With a Master of Arts in Sound Design and over a decade of experience in game development, AR/VR, and immersive media, I bring sound to life across platforms and genres.

My passion lies in crafting adaptive, dynamic soundscapes that elevate storytelling and enhance player immersion. From ambient design to interactive music systems and voiceover implementation, I handle audio pipelines from concept to integration—always aiming for impactful, cohesive sonic experiences.

When I’m not designing audio, I’m geeking out over game engines, solving puzzles, or rocking out to Dream Theater. I’m also a proud dad, juggling bedtime stories and boss battles.

Let’s bring worlds to life with sound!

Interactive Audio Specialist, Epic Stock Media

At Epic Stock Media, I leveraged extensive knowledge in field recording sessions to create high-quality sound assets. My responsibilities included mixing and mastering bulk audio assets, labeling and organizing files for optimal workflow, and creating thematic sound effects libraries tailored specifically for video game sound designers.

Oct 2014 - May 2019

Sound Designer, Freelance

As a freelance sound designer, I built custom interactive audio systems and integrated audio middleware to enable adaptive sound behaviors based on game parameters. My work encompassed dialog design, from production to integration, and creating audio for cutscenes and cinematic sequences. I also developed interactive music systems using horizontal, vertical, and DSP methods to respond dynamically to game logic, player stats, and object proximity. Additionally, I contributed to prototyping, testing, debugging, and conducting audio QA, while collaborating closely with other disciplines like art, code, and game design throughout the development process.
